Indefinite Articles
  • Un is 'a,' masculine.
  • Una is 'a,' feminine.
  • Unos means 'some' or 'a few, ' masculine.
  • Unas means 'some' or 'a few, ' feminine.
Una/Unas
  • ending with 'a'
  • ending with 'dad' [like ciudad(city), libertad(freedom)]
  • ending with 'ión' [like profesión]
exceptions: mano(hand), flor(flower), carne(meat), leche(milk), clase(class)

Un/Unos
  • ~ rest ~
exceptions: problema(problem), microondas(microwave), sal(salt)

Plurals
  • words ending with vowel +s [bolsa -> bolsas]
  • words ending with consonent +es [reloj -> relojes]
  • words ending with 'z' -ces [lápiz -> lápiices]

Tener Que [Have to]

The verb, tener que, is the way to say that we need to or have to do something.

  • I have to study. Tengo que estudiar.
  • I have to go. Tengo que ir.
  • You have to study. Tienes que estudiar/Tiene que estudiar.
  • He has to work. Tiene que trabajar.
  • We have to study. Tenemos que estudiar.
  • They have to work. Tienen que trabajar.

Ser vs Estar

The verb estar also translates into English as "to be". While ser is preferred for more permanent situations, estar tends to be used for passing ones. To be more specific, estar is generally used for things like position, location, action, condition and emotion (often memorized with the acronym PLACE).
